Thyroid

What is Thyroid?

The thyroid is a small, butterfly-shaped gland located at the base of the neck, just below the Adam's apple. The thyroid gland makes two main hormones: thyroxine (T-4) and triiodothyronine (T-3). These hormones affect every cell in the body. They support the rate at which the body uses fats and carbohydrates.

The thyroid gland is controlled by the pituitary gland in the brain, which produces thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) to regulate the thyroid's hormone production. When the thyroid gland produces too much or too little hormone, it can lead to various health problems, such as hyperthyroidism or hypothyroidism, respectively.

The common symptoms to diagnose thyroid yourself

Effects of thyroid

1.Fatigue and Weakness: Both hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can cause fatigue, weakness, and lethargy due to disruptions in energy metabolism and hormone imbalances.
2.Changes in Body Temperature: Thyroid disorders can affect how the body regulates temperature. Hypothyroidism is often associated with feeling cold, while hyperthyroidism can cause heat intolerance and excessive sweating.
3. Changes in Skin and Hair: Thyroid disorders can affect the skin and hair. Hypothyroidism may cause dry, rough skin, brittle nails, and hair loss, while hyperthyroidism can lead to sweaty or oily skin, hair thinning, and brittle nails.
4. Muscle and Joint Pain: Thyroid disorders can cause muscle weakness, stiffness, and joint pain. Hypothyroidism may lead to generalized muscle aches and stiffness, while hyperthyroidism can cause muscle weakness and tremors.
5.Digestive Issues: Thyroid dysfunction can impact digestion and bowel function. Hypothyroidism may lead to constipation, bloating, and slow digestion, while hyperthyroidism can cause diarrhea and increased bowel movements.
